Introduction

Over the past few decades, vast amounts of climate data have been collected from scientists, governments, academic institutions, and private companies. NASA's Greenhouse Gas Center makes this data publicly available, but the challenge is to interpret and present it in a way that motivates action. Through this website, we use data to tell stories about climate change impacts, such as rising temperatures, melting ice sheets, and more intense storms.

Climate Stories

New York City: Rising sea levels threaten the coastlines, especially in major cities like New York. By 2050, sea levels may rise significantly.

Louisiana: The low-lying areas of Louisiana are highly susceptible to flooding due to rising sea levels and increased storm intensity.

Texas: Texas experiences extreme heatwaves, droughts, and intense hurricanes, contributing to significant environmental and economic impacts.

California: Wildfires and prolonged droughts in California have become more frequent due to rising temperatures and changing weather patterns.

Take Action

Now that you’ve explored the data, it’s time to act. Here’s what you can do to contribute to a healthier planet:

  • Reduce your carbon footprint by using energy-efficient products.
  • Support policies and leaders who prioritize climate action.
  • Participate in community climate initiatives.
  • Educate others about the importance of climate change action.
